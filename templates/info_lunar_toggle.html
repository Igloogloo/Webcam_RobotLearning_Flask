Tufts University - Task Description and Controls

Virtual Agents learning from human interaction

Below is a description of the task you will be asked to complete for this study:

What is the task?

On the next page you will be asked to train a program to land a rocket on the landing pad. In this control problem the computer will be able to perform 4 actions: fire left engine, fire right engine, fire main (forward engine), and do nothing to move toward the landing pad. Your goal is to teach the program to land between the goal posts with both legs on the ground while using the engines a minimal amount. The landing pad and rocket will always start at the same location.

Lunar Lander Demo

What are the teaching controls?

You will be able to teach the program to complete the task using a “positive,” “negative,” and “off” toggle buttons. When the toggle is set to “positive” the program will receive continuous positive feedback that makes it more likely to repeat its current action. The program will only stop receiving positive feedback once the “negative” or “off” toggle is selected. When the toggle is set to “negative” the program will receive continuous negative feedback that makes it less likely to repeat its current action. The program will only stop receiving negative feedback once the “positive” or “off” toggle is selected. If the “off” toggle is selected the program will receive no feedback from the user on its actions until the “positive” or “negative” toggle is selected.

Important Note

On the next screen, a small duplicate window outside of your browser will pop-up. Please click anywhere on the webpage and the duplicate window will go away. After the duration of the study the window will automatically close. Thank you.

Duration

The interaction will last for approximately 10 minutes. After 10 minutes the external window will close and you will be prompted to complete the study.
